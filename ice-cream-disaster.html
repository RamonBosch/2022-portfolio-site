Ice Cream Disaster

2022 | Mobile game development and design

Ice Cream Disaster is the first game I've ever fully developed and released using Unreal Engine and Blender. UI design, 3D modeling, programming, soundtrack and marketing, I did it all by myself in a span of nine months.

The resulting game has been downloaded by more than 7K users between both platforms and has been reviewed by more than a hundred users with an average score of 4.9. You can download it on your Android or iOS device to try it!

Players and characters

Players came along after deciding which levels I would develop. It made sense to have a lost tourist as a character on a volcanic island or a slacker dinosaur mascot in an amusement park. Varying height, shape and colour, I managed to design nine simple and fun players that, even when reusing face expressions, looked very different from each other.

Ice cream cones

Cones can be unlocked and are used to catch ice cream scoops as they fall. Some of them have special abilities that make the game easier.

Level backgrounds

There are nine unlockable levels that signify the player's advance through the game. When deciding which stages the player would visit to beat the game, I asked myself, which could be fun places to catch ice cream scoops?

Ice cream flavours

Ice cream scoops come in different flavours. Some are very common and worth little, but others are rare, hard to find and very valuable. One of the secondary goals of the game is collecting all of the more than 60 different flavours available through the levels.

Obstacles and power-ups

Funny things that may fall on your ice cream. Obstacles will mess everything up, while power-ups improve your score or your cone's balance. Obstacles can melt, smash or abduct the ice cream scoops, while some power-ups or cones may prevent it.

DEMO materials

I released an early four-level demo with the idea of using other player's feedback to improve and debug the game. I also designed a few assets to promote it with the little materials I had so far.

UI and icons

Like many other elements in the game, the user interface went through a simplification process in which only the most essential elements of the game remained.

Game development

During the first week of the development phase, I created a quick prototype version of the game to test if it was fun. It was fun! So I went further and added a point counter, a cone button, a score screen, a menu... and slowly built Ice Cream Disaster.

Promotional illustrations

Using tweaked game sprites and concept art, I designed a series of promotional illustrations to showcase the game's themes and feel to new players.

Social media content

In order to build brand hype, attract as many players as possible before launch and become visible to search engines, I started publishing content on Twitter, Instagram, Tiktok, Youtube, Reddit and Facebook seven months before the release of the game.

Trailers and adverts

Finally, I put together four adverts / trailers combining gameplay recordings, social media content, in-game animations and After Effects sequences to pique new player's interest after the release of the game.
