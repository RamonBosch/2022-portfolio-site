Chilly Chuck

SUMMARY

I like to come up with new fictional game concepts and sketch ideas about them. Chilly Chuck was one of my favourite concepts from an old notebook and a few years ago I had the time to develop it a little bit further.

Chilly Chuck would be a turn-based isometric puzzle game heavily oriented towards resource management. The player would use expendable supplies to melt the ice, defeat foes and advance. Wasting food carelessly may cause getting stuck and having to redo the stage again using a different strategy.

Story

Chuck is the only inhabitant left in Frozenville. Winter comes early as usual, but this year, instead of hibernating with his stash of ridiculously strong flavoured chilly peppers, he decides to travel the tundra to spend the winter on paradise island Sunland. A simple two-scene animation shows us the premise of the story.

It's winter in Frozenville and Chuck has nothing but a cabin full of chilly peppers to pass the season... Oh boy, so spicy!

Enough of this! Chuck packs his stuff and parts to Sunland where the weather is bright and the food is sweet. Will he find a way through the freezing tundra?

Power-ups

Hot pepper: Chuck exhales red hot fire breath in three directions, burning nearby enemies.

Electro-pepper: Chuck shoots an electric bolt that damages enemies in front of him.

Pepper bomb: Chuck explodes and damages all enemies behind him and by his side.

Foes

Snowmoles love to hoard chilly peppers of any kind. If Chuck's not quick enough, he may have to enter their mole holes to retrieve his food.

Count Cold is constantly looking for warmth, so he hugs everything he finds, even moles or peppers.

Chuck will get a cold and lose his powers if Count Cold hugs him. A new chilly pepper will bring him back to normal.

Other items

Some recurring elements are also found in the game. Chilly boxes contain one or more types of chilly peppers. Ice blocks have to be melted or pushed to unblock the path. Route to Sunland signs mark the end of the stage.

Slides can be used to hit foes in a straight line. Wooden bridges have to be crossed quickly before they collapse.

Final game

I also designed a cartoony logo following the style of the game. The word Chilly is blue with icy reflections while the name Chuck is red hot with a gradient that suggests rising temperature.

And finally, a screenshot of a stage. To beat the game, the player has to go through all of the stages and get to Sunland. The number of chilly peppers per stage is limited, the user has to eat and employ them carefully to avoid getting stuck.

Each stage is a different puzzle-like series of icy cliffs that will make the player think about strategies to successfully get to the goal. At some point it won‚Äôt be possible to go back, so it is better to advance carefully.

In conclusion, I think Chilly Chuck could work as a fun game. If I ever find the time, I would love to build a simple demo to test and make sure if it is worth developing as a full game.
